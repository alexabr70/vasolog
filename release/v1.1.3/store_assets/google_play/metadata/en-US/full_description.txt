<b>Raynaud's disease tracker</b> for cold hands, color changes and circulation issues. Log every attack in seconds, identify your triggers and share a PDF report with your rheumatologist. Also called Raynaud's phenomenon or Raynaud's syndrome.

VasoLog is the symptom diary built specifically for people living with Raynaud's (primary or secondary). Whether you were just diagnosed or have managed cold fingers, white-blue-red color phases and numbness for years - clear data leads to better care.

── TRACK EVERY ATTACK ──
Log each episode in seconds: which fingers are affected, the color phase (white ischemia → blue cyanosis → red reperfusion), severity on the validated RCS scale (0-10) and duration. Add a photo and personal notes for complete documentation.

── AUTOMATIC WEATHER DATA ──
Every episode is automatically paired with real-time weather: temperature, humidity, atmospheric pressure and wind speed - captured at your exact location. No manual entry needed. See exactly how cold weather and pressure changes provoke your attacks.

── IDENTIFY YOUR TRIGGERS ──
Select from common triggers: cold exposure, emotional stress, vibration, smoking, caffeine and more. Over time, VasoLog reveals patterns - showing you exactly what provokes your episodes so you can avoid them.

── CHARTS & PATTERN INSIGHTS ──
The dashboard displays your attack frequency over the last 7 days and your monthly trigger breakdown. Spot patterns, track your progress and understand how seasons affect your circulation.

── PDF REPORT FOR YOUR DOCTOR ──
Generate a professional symptom report covering any date range with one tap. Share it instantly with your rheumatologist, vascular specialist or GP - saving consultation time and helping your doctor make informed decisions about your condition.

── YOUR DATA STAYS PRIVATE ──
All health data is stored locally on your device. No cloud, no account, no third-party tracking. Complete privacy - your symptom diary belongs to you alone.

── HOME SCREEN WIDGET ──
Keep your last episode summary visible on your home screen for quick reference.
